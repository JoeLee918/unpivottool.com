Advanced Sales Data Scenarios

Multi-Level Sales Data

When you have both quantity and revenue columns:

Product | Jan_Qty | Jan_Rev | Feb_Qty | Feb_Rev
Widget A | 100 | 15000 | 120 | 18000

Solution: Unpivot in two steps:

  1. Unpivot quantity columns (Jan_Qty, Feb_Qty) â†’ Product | Month | Quantity
  2. Unpivot revenue columns (Jan_Rev, Feb_Rev) â†’ Product | Month | Revenue
  3. Join the results on Product + Month

Quarterly Sales with Subtotals

Remove subtotal rows before unpivoting:

  1. Filter out rows containing "Total", "Subtotal", or "Average"
  2. Ensure only data rows remain
  3. Proceed with standard unpivot process

Common Sales Data Challenges

Mixed Data Types

Problem: Some cells contain text like "N/A" or "TBD"

Problem: Headers like "Jan-2024", "February", "Q1"

Solution: Standardize headers to consistent format before unpivot

Merged Cells in Headers

Problem: Quarterly headers spanning multiple months

Solution: Unmerge cells and fill in missing header values

FAQs

Should I unpivot all sales data? Not always. Keep wide format for executive summaries, use long format for detailed analysis.

How do I handle missing sales data? Decide whether to treat as zero sales or exclude from analysis. Document your approach consistently.

Can I unpivot sales data with multiple metrics? Yes, but consider unpivoting each metric separately then joining the results.

What's the best tool for large sales datasets? Power Query handles most business datasets well. For very large data, consider database tools or Python/R.
